# For people who understand what AI projects actually need.

Not another company that promises AI expertise and then counts CVs with buzzwords.

We're looking for people who know AI projects from the inside: what works, what fails – and why. Who understand the difference between a model that works technically and a solution that actually delivers value to the business.

---

## Who we're looking for

### Product Owner – AI Projects

You've managed backlogs that were more than to-do lists. You know how to translate a vision into deliverable increments – and how to explain why a sprint produced no result that feels like progress but isn't.

In AI projects, that means holding the business side and the development team together when requirements are still fuzzy and model quality is still inconsistent. Setting priorities when everything seems equally urgent.

---

### Business / Data Analyst – AI Projects

You translate. Between what the business needs and what the data can support. Between what a model can do and what a process can absorb.

You know BPMN, can structure requirements – and you know when a data foundation is good enough for an AI initiative and when it isn't. You ask the questions others don't dare to.

---

### Agile AI Developer

You build. With modern frameworks, LLMs, RAG architectures – and with the understanding that working software isn't automatically useful software.

You work iteratively, value feedback from the business side, and can explain technical decisions in a way a project manager actually understands. No ivory tower. No code nobody can operate.

---

## What we offer

No corporate structure. No politics. Projects where your work is directly visible.

We work with experienced clients who know what they want – and with a standard that goes beyond delivery: proving value, not just counting features.

---

## How to apply

No cover letter. No certificates first.

Write us a short note: what are you working on right now? What interests you about AI projects – and what frustrates you? The rest follows in conversation.
