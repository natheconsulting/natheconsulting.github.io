Projects leave traces. The most important one isn't the outcome – it's the judgement that develops when you've guided an initiative through its critical phases.

The following references come from over two decades of project practice: in public administration, industry, transport and the media sector. All client and project names are anonymised for reasons of confidentiality.

---

## AI Platforms and Machine Learning

**AI chatbot for career and education information**
*Large federal authority | 2025 | Risk Manager*

An AI chatbot providing information on several thousand occupations and delivering nationwide data on training, education and study options. Responsibilities included independent risk management, risk analysis, measure tracking and reporting directly to the steering committee.

---

**AI platform for 70,000 employees**
*Large federal authority | 2025–2026 | Risk Manager*

The organisation's central GenAI platform: knowledge management chatbot, text assistant and controlling chatbot in a single system, rolled out to over 70,000 employees. Risk management was conducted independently of the project line – with direct reporting to the steering committee, including when findings were uncomfortable.

---

**Digital user journey with AI integration**
*Large federal authority | 2024 – ongoing | Risk Manager*

Development of an end-to-end digital user journey for young people navigating career choices, optimised through GenAI use cases. Implementation was structured around agile fixed-price contracts for individual components. Risk management focused on the coordinated steering of multiple parallel delivery streams.

---

**Machine learning for automated job posting recognition**
*Large federal authority | 2023–2024 | Risk Manager*

An ML solution that automatically recognises, structures and transfers job postings from unstructured formats – emails, PDFs, free text. The quality metrics of the ML model (F1 score) were continuously monitored in direct alignment with project progress. The product was delivered on time – through close quality assurance and consistent change management.

## Pilots and Project Definition Phases

**Process automation – Stage 1: Data management**
*Large transport company | 2023 – ongoing | Management Consultant*

A multi-stage initiative to optimise a central planning process. The first stage focused on designing an IT solution for effective data management; the second stage covers progressive process automation. Work included current-state process documentation in BPMN, identification of automation potential and delivery oversight.

---

**Process automation pilot**
*Swiss federal authority | 2020–2021 | Management Consultant & Project Manager*

The goal was to define requirements and approach for future process automations and validate them through a pilot. Suitable pilot processes were selected in close collaboration with business process management; automation potential and project risks were assessed systematically. The pilot was presented to senior management in a decision paper – as the basis for commissioning the implementation project.

---

**Project definition phase: automation of an administrative process**
*Large federal authority | 2019 | Management Consultant*

Evaluation of how an automation project for a complex administrative process could be successfully structured. Design alternatives were developed and assessed collaboratively between the business side and the IT provider. Based on an analysis of organisational obstacles, senior management was advised against proceeding – one of the most important outcomes of a project review: the right decision at the right time.

---

**Feasibility study: process and decision automation**
*Large federal authority | 2018 | Management Consultant*

Feasibility assessment for the automation of decision-making processes in social law. Business rules were modelled in BPMN 2.0 and DMN 1.1 and tested in an execution environment. The study demonstrated both the value and the acceptance of the approach among business and IT stakeholders – laying the foundation for the subsequent implementation project.

## IT Transformation and System Integration

**IT transformation: replacing a critical application domain**
*Federal authority | 2003–2011 | Project Manager & Solution Architect*

Co-lead of an eight-year transformation programme to replace a mainframe-based application landscape in a security-sensitive environment. Multiple leadership changes on the client side, cross-departmental conflicts and high organisational complexity characterised the initiative. All components were delivered on time – including a fully redesigned solution architecture and an integrated process engine.

---

**Workflow-based ERP solution**
*International film rights trading company | 1999–2001 | Project Manager & Solution Architect*

Development and implementation of a custom ERP system for an internationally active film rights trader. All business processes were analysed, optimised and controlled end-to-end via a workflow engine. Project delivery under a fixed-price contract – with full accountability for cost, schedule, resources and sign-off by the client's management board.
